What Is a Vegan Diet?

A formal heuristic evaluation of ABK Learning Solutions’ “Featured Project,” using Nielsen’s frameworks to identify usability catastrophes and instructional friction points.

Client

ABK Learning Solutions, LLC

Heuristic Evaluation UX Audit

Context & Goals

ABK Learning Solutions, LLC was founded by Katie St. Pierre in 2022 to provide instructional design and learner experience services. This evaluation focuses on their "Featured Project"—a Storyline 360 module built for Antony’s Ristorante to reduce customer complaints by improving staff understanding of vegan diets.

I performed a structured audit to uncover critical friction points in the learner journey, ensuring the module effectively balances instructional goals with a seamless user experience.

Methodology

The audit was conducted using Jakob Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics. Each identified issue was tagged with a severity score to prioritize development efforts:

  1. Visibility of system status
  2. Match between system and real world
  3. User control and freedom
  4. Consistency and standards
  5. Error prevention
  6. Recognition rather than recall
  7. Flexibility and efficiency of use
  8. Aesthetic and minimalist design
  9. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors
  10. Help and documentation

Scale: 1 (Cosmetic), 2 (Minor Usability), 3 (Major Usability), 4 (Usability Catastrophe).

Key Findings & Recommendations

1. Lack of Time Visibility Severity 2

Heuristic: Visibility of System Status

Learners have no indication of elapsed or remaining time during video segments. This creates uncertainty regarding the time commitment required for the current section.

Recommendation: Add a time display or progress indicator beneath the video player to maintain learner orientation.

2. Inconsistent Error Logic Severity 3

Heuristic: Error Prevention & Recovery

Error messages inappropriately trigger even when the learner responds correctly. This breaks the "Match between system and real world" and undermines trust in the assessment feedback.

Recommendation: Audit Storyline trigger logic to ensure feedback only displays for incorrect responses and explains specifically how to recover.

3. Missing Assessment "Emergency Exit" Severity 3

Heuristic: User Control and Freedom

Once an assessment page is submitted in the book-style layout, learners cannot navigate backward to amend a choice, even if they recognize a mistake immediately. This lacks a necessary "emergency exit" for user errors.

Recommendation: Implement a "Back" button on the left page of the book layout to allow for amendments during the assessment phase.

4. Fragmented Results Feedback Severity 2

Heuristic: Consistency and Standards

The final results screen uses a fragmented sentence structure ("Your... Passing... Result: You did not") that is awkward to read and confusing for non-native English speakers.

Recommendation: Redesign the results page to follow standard left-to-right reading conventions and increase font size for clarity.

SEO & UX Alignment

This audit serves as a roadmap for improving engagement metrics that search engines and LMS analytics value:

  • Reduced Bounce Rates: Adding user controls (Back buttons) reduces frustration-driven exits.
  • Higher Completion Rates: Clear status visibility (Time displays) improves psychological pacing and completion likelihood.
  • Inclusivity: Standardizing microcopy and sentence structure ensures content quality for a diverse, global workforce.
