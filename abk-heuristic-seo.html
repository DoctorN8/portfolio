ABK Learning Solutions – Heuristic & SEO-Aligned UX Evaluation

Heuristic evaluation of the “What Is a Vegan Diet?” training module and ABK’s site experience, using Nielsen’s heuristics and SEO-aware content analysis to surface usability issues and actionable fixes.

Project Type
Heuristic Evaluation UX & Accessibility Content & SEO
Client

ABK Learning Solutions, LLC

Visit site

Context & Goals

ABK Learning Solutions is an eLearning development company that designs instructional content, research-backed learning experiences, and analytics-informed programs for clients with diverse training needs.

One featured project is a Storyline 360 module titled “What Is a Vegan Diet?”, built for restaurant staff at Antony’s Ristorante to reduce customer complaints by improving employees’ understanding of vegan options.

I was engaged to perform a structured heuristic evaluation of the training, using Nielsen’s ten usability heuristics and a severity rating scale to identify issues that affect learner experience, comprehension, and completion.

“Use a fast, systematic heuristic pass to uncover critical friction points, then translate them into specific, implementable changes for the design and development team.”

Methodology

[Image illustrating the 10 Nielsen Heuristics as a circular infographic]

Framework

The evaluation used Jakob Nielsen’s ten usability heuristics, including visibility of system status, user control and freedom, consistency and standards, flexibility and efficiency of use, error prevention, and recognition rather than recall.

Each identified issue was tagged with one or more violated heuristics and assigned a severity score on a 1–4 scale: 1 = Cosmetic problem, 2 = Minor usability problem, 3 = Major usability problem, 4 = Usability catastrophe.

Process

  • Reviewed the training flow end-to-end from the learner’s perspective, including introduction, practice, and assessment.
  • Logged each usability issue with a short description, violated heuristic, and severity rating.
  • Drafted targeted recommendations designed to be realistic to implement within the existing Storyline framework.

Selected Findings & Recommendations

[Image showing a sample of the audit spreadsheet with severity ratings and heuristic tags]

1. Video Time Visibility Severity:⭐⭐ (2 – Minor)

Heuristic Violated: Visibility of System Status. Learners had no clear indication of how much time remained while watching the video segment, causing uncertainty about the commitment required.

Recommendation: Add a time display or progress indicator beneath the video player showing either elapsed or remaining time.

2. Assessment Error Messages Severity:⭐⭐⭐ (3 – Major)

Heuristics Violated: Error Prevention, Recognition Rather Than Recall. Some error messages appeared even when learners answered correctly, undermining trust in the feedback system.

Recommendation: Audit trigger logic to ensure messages trigger only for incorrect responses, and clearly explain why an answer was wrong.

3. Lack of “Back” Option in Assessment Severity:⭐⭐⭐ (3 – Major)

Heuristic Violated: User Control and Freedom. Once an assessment page was submitted, learners could not navigate backward to correct inadvertent clicks.

Recommendation: Add a “Back” button to act as an emergency exit and support revisiting previous items when appropriate.

SEO & Content Alignment

While this was a usability review, the findings map directly to quality signals valued in modern SEO, such as clarity, user intent alignment, and helpfulness.

  • Clear feedback supports user satisfaction and reduces abandonment, influencing engagement metrics.
  • Consistent language and readable microcopy help users evaluate on-page value quickly.
  • Status visibility and navigation reduce friction, making it more likely users complete the intended journey.

Outcomes & Next Steps

The evaluation delivered a prioritized list of issuesPairing specific recommendations with severity scores enabled the development team to focus on high-impact changes first.

Future iterations can incorporate quantitative analytics—such as completion rates and time-on-task—to measure the impact of fixes over time and inform future module development.
