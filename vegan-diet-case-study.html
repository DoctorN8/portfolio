What is a Vegan Diet?

An instructional design and eLearning development project created to help restaurant staff identify vegan and non-vegan ingredients accurately, improve service confidence, and reduce customer complaints tied to dietary misunderstandings.

Experience the Project

Project Snapshot

Audience: Waitstaff and cook staff at Antony’s Ristorante.

Responsibilities: Instructional design, storyboard/design documentation, eLearning development, graphic design, and video design.

Tools: Articulate Storyline, WellSaid Labs, Toonly, Canva.

Overview

Antony’s Ristorante was experiencing customer dissatisfaction tied to two connected issues: staff lacked confidence when answering ingredient questions, and the kitchen was inconsistently meeting the needs of guests following vegan dietary patterns.

Those issues were affecting both trust and retention. The training solution was designed to help front-of-house and back-of-house staff recognize vegan ingredients, respond accurately to customer questions, and make more reliable decisions in service situations where dietary precision mattered.

The larger goal was to reduce complaints, improve consistency, and support a better dining experience for an expanding group of customers whose dietary needs were not being served well by existing staff knowledge.

Audience

Restaurant waitstaff and cook staff who needed fast, practical, job-relevant training.

Instructional Need

Build ingredient literacy and decision-making accuracy in real customer service contexts.

Success Goal

Reduce dietary-related service errors and improve customer confidence in staff responses.

Design Approach

The module was built as a focused performance-oriented learning experience rather than a broad nutrition course. The emphasis was on what staff needed to notice, remember, and apply during service interactions: which ingredients are vegan, where confusion is likely, and how to respond with confidence when customer questions arise.

I handled the instructional design work from planning through implementation, including design documentation, eLearning development, visual treatment, and media support. The final course was designed to be accessible, easy to navigate, and practical enough to support both awareness and applied decision-making on the job.

Highlights

01
Animated characters for storytelling

The module uses animated characters to guide learners through scenarios and explanations, helping the content feel more relatable and easier to remember.

02
Multiple practice opportunities

Learners encounter repeated opportunities to apply what they learned through interactive practice, helping strengthen recall and confidence.

03
Accessibility support

The design includes transcripts and closed captioning so the module is more inclusive and easier to access across learner needs.

04
Integrated glossary

A built-in glossary supports comprehension by defining key terms learners may need during the course or while revisiting concepts later.

05
Application-focused assessment

Assessment questions were designed to push beyond recall and encourage learners to apply ideas in realistic decision-making situations.
