Melbourne Lodge No. 143 – Local SEO & Content Structure

Local SEO and content-structure audit for Melbourne Lodge No. 143, focusing on meeting-info visibility, NAP consistency, and visitor-friendly navigation for a fraternal organization.

Client

Melbourne Lodge No. 143, F&A.M.

West Melbourne, Florida (District 17, Brevard County)

Visit Lodge Website
Tags
Local SEO Content Structure NAP Consistency Fraternal Organization

1. Context & Goals

Melbourne Lodge No. 143 is a Masonic lodge in West Melbourne, Florida, serving Brevard County and operating within District 17. The lodge website provides information about meetings, officers, history, and Masonic education.

The primary goal of this audit was to improve local discoverability and clarity for three key audiences: prospective members searching how to become a Mason in Melbourne, visiting brothers seeking lodge times and directions, and community members looking for information about Masonic activities.

Local organizations need search results that answer “who, what, where, and when” at a glance—meeting times and location matter more than marketing copy.

Current Strengths

  • Professional Wix design with clear top-level navigation and a welcoming message from the Worshipful Master.
  • Embedded Google Map on the contact page for visual directions.
  • Active presence on external platforms such as Grand Lodge listings and map sites.

Key Challenges

  • Generic page titles lacking local keywords and lodge number emphasis.
  • Missing meta descriptions across core pages, reducing click-through from search.
  • Critical meeting information buried in the footer or deeper pages rather than above the fold.
  • Minor inconsistencies in how name, address, and phone (NAP) appear across web properties.

2. Audit Findings

Homepage

On the homepage, the current title “Free and Accepted Masons - Melbourne Lodge 143” mentions both the fraternity and lodge number, but does not clearly specify location or meeting schedule in the search snippet. The main on-page heading is a Worshipful Master’s message, which is meaningful contextually but not ideal as the primary H1 for search and scanning.

  • Lodge name and number are visible in the header, but meeting times and address are only reliably present in the footer.
  • No meta description is set, leaving search engines to pull arbitrary text for snippets.

Contact Page

The contact page title includes “Contact | Free and Accepted Masons - Melbourne Lodge 143,” and the layout places the contact form above the fold with a “LOCATE US” heading and Google Map embedded below. The address appears in the map popup, but the meeting schedule and phone number are not prominently emphasized.

  • Critical “when we meet” information is missing from the page content itself.
  • The phone number is not in a prominent, click-to-call format near the top of the page.

External Listings & NAP Consistency

The Grand Lodge of Florida listing for Melbourne Lodge No. 143 is accurate and considered authoritative: it lists the lodge address at 2300 Dairy Rd, West Melbourne, FL 32904-5245, phone 321-831-3415, and the meeting schedule as 1st and 3rd Mondays at 7:30 PM.

Across the lodge website, map listings, and external references, there are minor formatting differences in address and phone, which are typical but worth standardizing for local SEO consistency.

4. Local SEO & NAP Consistency

Local SEO relies heavily on consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) information across the lodge website, Grand Lodge listing, and third-party directories.

[Image illustrating local citation and NAP consistency across different directory platforms]

Standard NAP Format

Name: Melbourne Lodge No. 143, F&A.M.
Street Address: 2300 Dairy Road, West Melbourne, FL 32904
Phone: (321) 831-3415
Website: https://www.melbournelodge143.com/

5. Technical SEO & Structured Data

To strengthen local signals, I recommended adding schema.org structured data and claiming Google Business Profile and Bing Places listings for the lodge.

Organization Schema

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Organization",
  "name": "Melbourne Lodge No. 143",
  "url": "https://www.melbournelodge143.com/",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "2300 Dairy Road",
    "addressLocality": "West Melbourne",
    "addressRegion": "FL",
    "postalCode": "32904"
  },
  "telephone": "+1-321-831-3415"
}
</script>

6. Content Strategy & Internal Linking

I identified content opportunities and internal link paths that support the core user journeys: visiting, joining, and learning about Freemasonry.

  • “Visiting Melbourne Lodge: What to Expect” – covers parking, dress code, and arrival time.
  • “How to Become a Mason in Melbourne, Florida” – explains requirements and petition process.

7. Implementation Priorities & Expected Outcomes

I organized recommendations into high, medium, and low priority tiers. The most impactful changes—titles, meta descriptions, homepage meeting box, and NAP standardization—could be implemented in roughly six hours.

With these changes plus claimed local listings, the lodge can reasonably expect increases in organic traffic and better visibility for local queries such as “Masonic lodge West Melbourne.”

8. Case Study Summary

This project focuses on a local, mission-driven organization. Melbourne Lodge’s needs revolve around clear meeting information, local citations, and respectful presentation of a fraternal body.

The core lesson is that local SEO for lodges is about honoring user intent: making “who, what, where, and when” effortless to find and aligning NAP everywhere to bridge the gap between online discovery and in-person visits.
